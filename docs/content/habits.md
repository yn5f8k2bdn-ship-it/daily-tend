# Habit library

Initial library of 24 habits — 8 per zone. Users pick one habit to track actively at any time. The rules engine may surface others as suggestions.

**Fields.**
- `name` — short, imperative. What appears on the chip.
- `zone` — one of `self`, `purpose`, `loved_ones`.
- `why_it_works` — one line, plain language. No "studies show", no stats.
- `suggested_cadence` — `daily`, `weekdays`, `weekly`.

**Balance notes.** Each zone has a mix of quick (≈5-min), medium, and connection-based habits. Not everything is solitary self-care.

## Self (8)

### 1. 10-min walk
- **zone.** self
- **why_it_works.** Moving for 10 minutes changes how your head feels. That's usually all you need to start.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 2. Glass of water on waking
- **zone.** self
- **why_it_works.** You're dehydrated from sleep. Water first, caffeine second.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 3. Lights down by 10pm
- **zone.** self
- **why_it_works.** Dim light is a signal your body actually listens to.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 4. 5 slow breaths before getting out of bed
- **zone.** self
- **why_it_works.** Starts the day without a cortisol spike. Takes under a minute.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 5. Step outside in the morning
- **zone.** self
- **why_it_works.** Even two minutes of outdoor light resets how tired you'll feel tonight.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 6. Put the phone in another room for dinner
- **zone.** self
- **why_it_works.** One hour where you're not on call. You'll notice the difference.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 7. Stretch for 5 minutes before bed
- **zone.** self
- **why_it_works.** Winds the body down faster than lying there scrolling.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 8. One proper meal a day
- **zone.** self
- **why_it_works.** Not every meal has to be a meal. One of them should be.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

## Purpose (8)

### 1. Plan tomorrow's top 3
- **zone.** purpose
- **why_it_works.** Three beats ten. You'll actually do the three.
- **suggested_cadence.** weekdays

### 2. 25 minutes on one thing, phone away
- **zone.** purpose

- **suggested_cadence.** weekdays

### 3. Close the laptop at a set time

- **why_it_works.** Work fills the time you give it. Give it less.
- **suggested_cadence.** weekdays

- **zone.** purpose

- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 5. Say no to one thing
- **zone.** purpose

- **suggested_cadence.** weekly

### 6. Weekly Friday review (10 min)
- **zone.** purpose
- **why_it_works.** Ten minutes on Friday saves you two hours of drift next week.
- **suggested_cadence.** weekly

### 7. First hour, no email
- **zone.** purpose
- **why_it_works.** Starting with your stuff, not someone else's, changes the whole day.
- **suggested_cadence.** weekdays

### 8. One small thing toward the bigger thing
- **zone.** purpose
- **why_it_works.** Big goals don't need big moves — they need small ones that don't stop.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

## Loved Ones (8)

### 1. Text someone I love
- **zone.** loved_ones
- **why_it_works.** Two lines is enough. Most people are glad to hear from you.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 2. Call a parent or sibling
- **zone.** loved_ones
- **why_it_works.** Calls feel bigger than texts, even short ones. Ten minutes is plenty.
- **suggested_cadence.** weekly

### 3. Ask my partner one real question
- **zone.** loved_ones
- **why_it_works.** "How are you?" mostly gets "fine". "What was hard today?" gets an answer.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 4. Put the phone away when the kids talk to me
- **zone.** loved_ones
- **why_it_works.** They'll remember whether you looked up. So will you.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 5. One properly undistracted hour at home
- **zone.** loved_ones
- **why_it_works.** Presence beats quantity. One real hour outweighs a day of being half-there.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 6. Organise something to look forward to
- **zone.** loved_ones
- **why_it_works.** Dinner, a walk, a coffee. The anticipation matters as much as the thing.
- **suggested_cadence.** weekly

### 7. Thank someone out loud
- **zone.** loved_ones
- **why_it_works.** Gratitude felt but not said doesn't travel. Say the sentence.
- **suggested_cadence.** daily

### 8. Reach out to a friend you've lost touch with
- **zone.** loved_ones
- **why_it_works.** The awkward part lasts ten seconds. The reconnection lasts longer.
- **suggested_cadence.** weekly

## Notes

- Cadences are suggestions, not rules. The app shouldn't penalise missed days of daily habits.
- Nothing in this list requires equipment, a subscription, or more than a few dollars.
- "Why it works" lines stay under 18 words. If one grows, trim it.
